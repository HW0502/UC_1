How to cook Korean Galbi


"Galbi" is the Korean word for "rib", and the dish is usually made with beef short ribs. When pork spareribs or another meat is used instead, the dish is named accordingly.
Galbi is served raw, then cooked on tabletop grills usually by the diners themselves. The dish may be marinated in a sweet and savory sauce usually containing soy sauce, garlic, and sugar. Both non-marinated and marinated galbi are often featured in Korean barbecue.

Ingredients

  • main: beef ribs 800g, nutritional leek 200g, enoki mushroom 300g, pear 1~2, onions 300g, sugar
  • sauce: water 1/4cup, soy sauce 3Tb, white wine 2Tb, garlic 3~4, sesame oil 1Tb, salt, pepper

Step 1

For ribs, wash the bones in running water to remove impurities. Soak in water for 30 minutes, drain the blood, remove moisture with a kitchen towel, and then put the sheath with the tip of a knife.
tip! If you immerse in coke for 10 minutes instead of water in the process of draining the blood, the meat will be soft and juicy.

Step 2

Grind the pears and onions on a grater and mix well with the rest of the ingredients. Stir the ribs for more than 10 minutes.

Step 3

Mix the ribs seasoning ingredients and cook the ribs for 3 hours or more in the refrigerator.

Step 4

Put the ribs on the preheated pan and cook them.

Step 5

Cut the leek into 5cm long, cut the top into 2 equal parts, and season with soy sauce, garlic, and sugar.

Step 6

Put the grilled ribs on the plate, sprinkle with chopped green onion and pine nuts, and add a nourishing leek salad.
